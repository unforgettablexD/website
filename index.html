Research

Research summary

My research interest is in sequential decision-making under uncertainty, especially when actions are shaped by execution frictions, risk limits, and partial observability.

Empirically, I work in market environments because they provide a demanding setting for reinforcement learning, stochastic modeling, and policy evaluation. Methodologically, I am interested in reinforcement learning, stochastic control, empirical asset pricing, and robust experimental design.

My aim for doctoral work is to study models and decision procedures that remain credible once realistic constraints are imposed, rather than only under simplified simulations.

Current questions include constrained RL evaluation, execution-aware simulation, and the stability of decision rules under regime shifts and risk controls.
